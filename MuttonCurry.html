Mutton Curry

Savor the flavors of tradition and innovation, one delicious recipe at a time

Preparation Time: 120 minutes

Total Servings: 4

Ingredients

Recipe

  1. In a large bowl, mix the mutton pieces with 1/2 cup of yogurt, 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste, 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour. Longer marination will enhance the flavor.
  2. Heat 4 tablespoons of cooking oil or ghee in a large pot or pressure cooker over medium heat.Add the whole spices: 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, 2 bay leaves, 1 small cinnamon stick, 3-4 cardamom pods, 4-5 cloves, and 5-6 black peppercorns. Sauté until fragrant.
  3. Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another 2-3 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
  4. Add the finely chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and oil starts to separate from the masala.
  5. Add 2 teaspoons of coriander powder and 1 teaspoon of cumin powder. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add the marinated mutton pieces to the pot. Cook on high heat for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mutton is browned.
  7. If using a pressure cooker, add 2-3 cups of water, cover the lid, and cook for about 20-25 minutes or until the mutton is tender. If using a regular pot, add the water, cover, and simmer on low heat for about 1-1.5 hours or until the mutton is cooked and tender.
  8. Once the mutton is cooked, check the seasoning and adjust salt if needed. Add 1 teaspoon of garam masala and the slit green chilies (if using). Let it simmer for another 5-10 minutes.
  9. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a splash of lemon juice (if desired).Serve the mutton curry hot with steamed rice, roti, naan, or any Indian bread of your choice!
