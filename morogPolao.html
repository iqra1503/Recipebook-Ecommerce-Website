Morog Polao

Savor the flavors of tradition and innovation, one delicious recipe at a time

Preparation Time: 60 minutes

Total Servings: 3

Ingredients

Recipe

  1. In a large bowl, mix the chicken pieces with 1/2 cup of yogurt, 2 tablespoons of ginger-garlic paste, 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, and salt to taste. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour, or preferably overnight in the refrigerator.
  2. Rinse 2 cups of basmati rice under running water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  3. Heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  4. Add the whole spices: 2 bay leaves, 1-2 cinnamon sticks, 4-5 cardamom pods, 4-5 cloves, 6-8 black peppercorns, 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds, mace, nutmeg, and star anise if using. Sauté until fragrant.
  5. Add 2 large thinly sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.Add 1 teaspoon each of garlic paste and ginger paste. Sauté for another 2-3 minutes.
  6. Add the marinated chicken to the pot. Cook on high heat for 5-7 minutes until the chicken is browned. Add the chopped tomato and cook until it becomes soft and oil starts to separate from the masala.
  7. Add 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of coriander powder, 1 teaspoon of cumin powder, and 1 teaspoon of garam masala powder. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  8. Add the soaked and drained rice to the pot. Stir gently to mix with the chicken and spices. Add 1/2 cup of milk and 4 cups of water. Add the slit green chilies.
  9. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  10. Once the rice is cooked, fluff it gently with a fork. If using saffron, sprinkle the saffron milk over the rice and let it sit for a few minutes before mixing.
