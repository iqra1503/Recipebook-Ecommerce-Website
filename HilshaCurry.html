Hilsha Curry

Savor the flavors of tradition and innovation, one delicious recipe at a time

Preparation Time: 150 minutes

Total Servings: 4

Ingredients

Recipe

  1. Clean the hilsa fish pieces and marinate them with a pinch of turmeric powder and salt. Set aside for 15-20 minutes.
  2. Heat 4 tablespoons of mustard oil in a large pan or kadai over medium heat until it starts to smoke. Reduce the heat and let it cool down a bit (this step helps to reduce the pungency of the mustard oil).
  3. Lightly fry the marinated hilsa pieces in the mustard oil until they are golden brown on both sides. Be careful not to over-fry. Remove the fish pieces and set them aside.
  4. In the same oil, add 1 large finely chopped onion and sauté until golden brown.Add 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste and sauté until the raw smell disappears (about 2 minutes).
  5. Add 2 medium finely chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and oil starts to separate from the masala.
  6. Add 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 teaspoon of red chili powder, 1 teaspoon of coriander powder, and 1 teaspoon of cumin powder. Mix well and cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  7. If using, add 1-2 tablespoons of mustard paste and cook for a minute.
  8. Add 2 cups of water (adjust for desired consistency) and the slit green chilies. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  9. Carefully add the fried hilsa pieces to the curry. Reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes or until the fish is cooked through and the flavors are well combined.
  10. Check the seasoning and adjust salt if needed.Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  11. Serve the hilsa fish curry hot with steamed rice. Enjoy your flavorful and aromatic hilsa fish curry!
